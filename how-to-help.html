here are some resources for at risk children

signs of abuse

  • Withdrawal from friends or usual activities
  • Changes in behavior — such as aggression, anger, hostility or hyperactivity — or changes in school performance
  • Depression, anxiety or unusual fears or a sudden loss of self-confidence
  • An apparent lack of supervision
  • Frequent absences from school or reluctance to ride the school bus
  • Reluctance to leave school activities, as if he or she doesn't want to go home
  • Attempts at running away
  • Rebellious or defiant behavior
  • Attempts at suicide

precautions

Make sure to keep calm and act carefully with the victim. Also keep in mind if what you do might potentially keep the child in a dangerous position. These situations are very tricky to navigate and you must be very careful to not upset the child or cause any damage.

when communicating with the child keep in mind:

  • Offer a drink or snack to make them comfortable
  • validate them and tell them you believe them
  • provide toys and child friendly enviroment
  • be patient with them if they do not wish to talk
  • understand what the child wants from you
  • use terminology the child will understand
  • stay kind, calm, and collected
